Practices of Sikhi

Daily Prayer and Meditation

Sikhs are expected to pray and meditate daily, usually in the morning and evening. This can involve recitation of hymns from the Guru Granth Sahib and meditation on the name of God.

Kesh and the Five Ks

Sikhs are required to keep their hair uncut as a sign of devotion and respect for the perfection of God's creation. They may also observe the Five Ks, which are five articles of faith that are worn at all times: kesh (uncut hair), kara (a steel bracelet), kanga (a wooden comb), kaccha (cotton underwear), and kirpan (a sword).

Seva and Langar

Sikhs believe in the importance of selfless service, or seva, and often engage in community service projects. They also practice the concept of langar, in which a communal meal is served to all who come to the gurdwara (Sikh place of worship), regardless of their background or ability to pay.
