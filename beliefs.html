Beliefs of Sikhi

The Concept of Waheguru

Sikhs believe in a single, all-pervading, supreme God, who is referred to as Waheguru. This term means "the wonderful teacher" and is used as a way of expressing awe and reverence for God. Sikhs believe that God is present in all things and that it is possible to experience a deep inner connection with God through meditation and prayer.

The Guru Granth Sahib

The Guru Granth Sahib is the holy scripture of Sikhi, and it is considered to be the eternal guru of the Sikhs. It is a collection of hymns and teachings written by the Gurus and other Sikh saints. Sikhs believe that the Guru Granth Sahib contains the wisdom and guidance necessary for leading a spiritual and meaningful life. It is given the highest respect and is treated with the utmost reverence in Sikh homes and temples.

Equality and Social Justice

Sikhi emphasizes the importance of equality and social justice for all people. The Gurus taught that all human beings are created equal and should be treated with dignity and respect, regardless of their religion, race, or social status. Sikhs are encouraged to work towards creating a just and fair society and to stand up against injustice wherever it may be found.

Service and Community

Sikhi places a strong emphasis on service and community. Sikhs are encouraged to serve others and to work towards the betterment of their communities. This can take many forms, such as volunteering, donating to charity, or simply being a good neighbor. Sikhs believe that by serving others, we can create a more harmonious and compassionate world.
