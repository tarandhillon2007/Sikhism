History of Sikhi

The Founding of Sikhi

Sikhi was founded in the 15th century by Guru Nanak, who rejected the caste system and the idolatry of Hinduism and Islam. He preached a message of devotion to one creator God and the importance of living a virtuous life.

Guru Nanak and the Subsequent Gurus

After Guru Nanak, nine gurus followed who built upon his teachings and worked to spread the religion and defend the rights of the Sikh people. These gurus are considered to be divinely inspired and their teachings are recorded in the Sikh holy scripture, the Guru Granth Sahib.

The Punjab Region and Sikh History

The history of Sikhi is closely tied to the history of the Punjab region and the struggles of the Sikh people. The religion has a long history of resistance to oppression and has played a significant role in the development of the Punjab region.
